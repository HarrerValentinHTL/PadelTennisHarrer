The Padel-Court

The court measures 20 meters in length and 10 meters in width, which is approximately one-third of the size of a tennis court. This smaller size facilitates a more social and engaging game, as players are closer to each other and the action is more concentrated. The surface of the padel court is typically made of artificial grass, which helps in slowing down the ball and making the game more accessible to players of all ages and skill levels. One of the distinctive features of the padel court is its walls. The back walls can be up to 4 meters high, and the side walls start at the same height but are reduced to 3 meters after the first 3 meters of length from the back wall. The front part of the court has no walls, which defines the playing area and influences how players develop their playing strategies. The balls used in padel are similar to those in tennis, though they have a slightly lower pressure to adapt to the enclosed playing environment and the wall bounces. The net divides the court into two equal halves and plays a central role in the game, similar to tennis. However, the scoring system in padel is the same as in tennis, making it easy for tennis players to understand padel scoring. The enclosure of the court by walls adds a unique twist to the game, as players must constantly adapt their shots and strategies based on the possibility of wall rebounds, which is not a factor in traditional tennis. This integration of walls into the game not only distinguishes padel from tennis but also adds a layer of complexity and fun, making it an engaging sport that combines elements of tennis, squash, and racquetball. The game is typically played in doubles, which emphasizes teamwork and strategy over individual prowess, making it a highly social and enjoyable sport for participants of all ages.
