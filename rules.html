Rules of Padel

Padel is a racket sport that blends elements of tennis and squash, played on an enclosed court about one-fourth smaller than a tennis court. The game is always played in doubles with a scoring system similar to tennis. Here's a streamlined overview of padel rules: The court is enclosed with walls, and the game uses a solid, stringless racket with balls that are similar to tennis balls but slightly less pressurized. Players serve underhand, and the ball must first bounce in the server's box before being hit diagonally across into the opponent's service box. Each player gets two attempts to make a valid serve. The ball can be played off the walls, like in squash, but it must bounce on the ground at least once before it can be hit. Players can volley the ball without letting it bounce, except on the serve. The objective is to win points by making the ball bounce twice in the opponent's court or by forcing opponents to commit errors. Matches are typically played in the best of three sets, with each set being won by the first team to reach six games with at least a two-game lead. If the score in a set reaches 6-6, a tie-break is often played to determine the winner of that set. Scoring follows the traditional tennis format of 15, 30, 40, and game, with a deuce occurring at 40-40. From deuce, a team must win two consecutive points to win the game. Faults occur when a player fails to serve correctly, the ball bounces more than once in their court, or the ball is hit out of bounds. The game encourages strategic play, utilizing the walls for defensive and offensive shots, making padel a dynamic and engaging sport for players of all levels.
